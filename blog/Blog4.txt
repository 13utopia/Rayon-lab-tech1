## Blog Content

Effective laboratory storage is not simply about adding more cabinets. It is about storing different materials in appropriate locations while maintaining accessibility, organisation, cleanliness, and safer working conditions.

A well-planned laboratory may use overhead storage cabinets, glassware storage cabinets, chemical storage cabinets, under-bench cabinets, reagent racks, and mobile storage units.

Each storage product should be selected according to its intended contents.

### Overhead Storage Cabinet

An overhead storage cabinet is generally installed above a laboratory working table or along the wall.

It can be used for lightweight, frequently required laboratory items that need to remain accessible without occupying the work surface.

Important factors include:

* Mounting strength
* Cabinet depth
* Door opening
* User reach
* Internal shelves
* Position above equipment
* Clearance from the worktop

Heavy items should not be stored at unsafe heights.

### Glassware Storage Cabinet

A glassware storage cabinet helps keep laboratory glassware organised and protected.

Its design may include adjustable shelves, transparent doors, internal partitions, or trays depending on the glassware type.

Before selecting the cabinet, consider:

* Glassware dimensions
* Weight
* Usage frequency
* Drying requirements
* Shelf spacing
* Visibility
* Cleaning requirements

### Chemical Storage Cabinet

A chemical storage cabinet should be selected according to the chemicals being stored and their compatibility.

Chemicals should not be grouped only because they fit inside the same cabinet. Storage planning must consider hazard classification, compatibility, ventilation requirements, containment, labelling, access control, and applicable safety procedures.

The cabinet material, internal shelves, trays, locks, and ventilation arrangement should suit the intended application.

### Under-Bench Storage

Under-bench cabinets make use of the area beneath laboratory working tables and island tables.

They may include:

* Drawers
* Hinged-door cabinets
* Removable cabinets
* Mobile pedestals
* Open equipment spaces

Storage should not block service connections, emergency access, or equipment ventilation.

### Benefits of Planned Laboratory Storage

Correct storage planning can support:

* Cleaner working surfaces
* Faster material access
* Better inventory organisation
* Reduced clutter
* Improved workflow
* Efficient use of space
* Easier cleaning and maintenance

### Conclusion

Different laboratory materials require different storage solutions. Overhead cabinets improve accessibility, glassware cabinets organise fragile items, chemical storage cabinets address application-specific safety needs, and under-bench cabinets maximise available space.
