## Blog Content

Modern laboratories use sensitive instruments for weighing, measuring, testing, analysing, and recording highly precise results.

External vibration, unstable working surfaces, inappropriate equipment placement, and difficult-to-clean furniture can affect laboratory efficiency and instrument performance.

Anti vibration tables and stainless steel laboratory furniture address different operational requirements within a laboratory.

### What Is an Anti Vibration Table?

An anti vibration table is a specialised laboratory table designed to provide a stable working surface for sensitive instruments.

It may be used with:

* Analytical balances
* Precision weighing equipment
* Microscopes
* Measurement instruments
* Testing devices
* Sensitive laboratory equipment

The table’s construction is developed to reduce the transfer of surrounding vibration to the equipment placed on its surface.

### Where Can Laboratory Vibration Come From?

Common sources include:

* Foot traffic
* Doors opening and closing
* Nearby machinery
* Ventilation equipment
* Compressors
* Pumps
* Moving trolleys
* Building movement
* Equipment installed on the same surface

The table should also be positioned away from unnecessary movement, airflow disturbance, heat sources, and vibrating machinery wherever possible.

### Benefits of an Anti Vibration Table

A correctly selected table can provide:

* A stable instrument platform
* Better isolation from surrounding movement
* Dedicated equipment space
* Improved operational consistency
* Safer equipment placement
* Better organisation around sensitive instruments

The equipment manufacturer’s installation requirements should always be reviewed before finalising table dimensions and location.

### Why Use Stainless Steel Laboratory Furniture?

Stainless steel furniture provides a smooth, durable, and easy-to-maintain surface for selected laboratory applications.

It may be suitable for healthcare, pharmaceutical, food-testing, research, and industrial environments where regular cleaning and hygienic working surfaces are important.

Common products include:

* Stainless steel working tables
* Stainless steel sink tables
* Instrument tables
* Storage cabinets
* Laboratory trolleys
* Equipment stands

### Is Stainless Steel Suitable for Every Laboratory?

Not necessarily. Furniture material should be selected according to chemical exposure, cleaning agents, temperature, moisture, hygiene requirements, and laboratory processes.

Some chemical applications may require alternative worktop or lining materials. The complete application should be assessed before manufacturing.

### Conclusion

An anti vibration table supports sensitive instruments by providing a stable and dedicated working surface. Stainless steel laboratory furniture supports selected applications requiring durability, hygiene, and easier maintenance.
